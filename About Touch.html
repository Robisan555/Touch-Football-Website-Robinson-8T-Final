Rules of Touch




Field of Play

  • The field is 70 metres from try-line to try-line and 50 metres from sideline to sideline.
  • If you step out of the boundary, there is a changeover.
  • A match is 40 minutes long, including two 20 minute halves and a half time break.
  • The Interchange Areas are located no closer than one metre from each sideline.
  • The standard playing field is made of grass.
  • The ball is oval and inflated. Must be approved by the TFA.

Ball Possession

  • Each team can have 6 touches before a changeover is applied. When a player gets touched by the defending team, they put the ball on the groud immediately and step over it. Another player, called the dummy half picks it up and play continues. The dummy half is not allowed to score a try and if they get tagged, the possesion changes.
  • If the ball is dropped, or the ball is passed forwards (towards the opposition try line) the a change of possession occurs.
  • After a touch, the defending team has to retreat by 7 metres, or retreat to the try-line.
  • A player cannot pass or deliver the ball after they have been touched.
  • A tap is made at the beginning of each half. You tap the ball on your foot and you can run. There is no dummy half.

Players

  • Everyone on the team must have matching uniforms.
  • Safe footwear must be worn. Hats and caps are allowed as long as they meet TFA regulations.
  • No type of jewellery is to be worn, and no sharp fingernails are allowed.
  • A team consits of maximum of 14 players, and only 6 are allowed on the field at a time. (a team must have at least 4 players on the field to continue)
  • In mixed gender competitions, the minimum male requirement is one and the minimum female requirement is one. There can only be a maximum of three males allowed on the Field at one time.

Origin of Touch Football

  • Touch football was one of the only sports that was founded in Australia. It has been formally recognised for 50 years already, and had its Golden Jubilee in 2018.
  • Touch football was originally invented as a method of training for Rugby League.
  • The founding fathers of Touch Football were Bob Dyke and Ray Vawdon, of the South Sydney Junior Rugby League Club.
  • As popularity for this sport rose, sport associations began to emerge. The most famous is the Australian Touch Association, which is now known as Touch Football Australia.
  • The rules, skills, concepts and strategies of the game have been evolving throughout the years and became an unique sport with many people enjoy.
  • Now, the sport is recommended for people of all ages and sporting abilities. It is recognised as an extremely social sport, one in which men and women can compete together.
